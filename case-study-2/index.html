02 — The Challenge

A growing business with no operational backbone

The client had built real traction — a 35,000+ subscriber email list, a Facebook community of 56,000 members, and an existing catalog of digital products — but the infrastructure running it all was fragmented and unmaintainable.

ClickFunnels

Sales funnels and course content delivery — aging and expensive relative to what it provided

ConvertKit

Email marketing with 35K+ subscribers — disorganized tags, cold segments, and no active cleanup strategy

WordPress

Main website — separate from funnels, creating fragmented customer journeys

Canva / GDrive

Assets scattered without a centralized content system or publishing workflow

No PM system

No structured project management — timelines, assignments, and approvals managed ad hoc

On top of stabilizing operations, the client had an ambitious goal: launch a brand-new 9-module course within the same 6-month window, while also standing up a sustainable weekly content engine for newsletters, blogs, and social media.

ClickFunnels + fragmented ops + no content system → MyCX + structured team + weekly content cadence + full course launch
03 — How I Structured It

Eight phases, running in parallel

This engagement required running platform migration, content operations, and launch preparation simultaneously — without dropping any live customer-facing systems in the process. Here's how I sequenced it.

Phase 0

Onboarding & Discovery

Full audit of existing tech stack, email list health, offer catalog, and content assets. Built the project tracker in ClickUp, established folder structures in Google Drive, and onboarded all team members with access and role clarity before any build work began.

Phase 1

Platform Migration (ClickFunnels → MyCX)

Migrated all funnels, course content, and product pages from ClickFunnels to MyCX — a GoHighLevel-based platform. Rebuilt automations, reconnected payment gateways, set up product fulfillment, and configured domains. Maintained zero disruption to existing students and customers throughout.

Phase 2

Email System Audit & Re-engagement

Audited the ConvertKit account — reviewed 50+ tags, identified 1,700+ cold subscribers for a re-engagement sequence, and restructured segmentation logic. Set up a new nurture sequence to run alongside the re-engagement campaign for newly warm contacts.

Phase 3

Content Systems & Calendar

Established a repeatable weekly content system: newsletter (monthly themes, recurring format), blog (2x per month, topic-mapped to the upcoming course), and social media (2–3x per week across Facebook and Instagram). Built the content calendar and assigned ownership to the graphic designer and copywriter.

Phase 4

Lead Magnet Funnel Builds

Built three new lead magnet funnels from scratch — an interactive quiz, a learning style assessment, and a resource ebook — each with landing pages, thank you pages, confirmation emails, and connecting ConvertKit automations. Also built the course waitlist funnel ahead of launch.

Phase 5

Course Build & Launch Prep

Managed the full build of the course inside MyCX — uploaded all content, configured drip schedules, and coordinated asset delivery (slides, workbooks, videos) across the graphic designer and video editor. Coordinated the copywriter on sales page, launch emails, abandoned cart sequences, and onboarding copy.

Phase 6

Launch Execution

Executed the full launch sequence: pre-launch teaser emails to the list, waitlist promotion, cart open and close, and post-purchase onboarding. Scheduled and published social media content in coordination with the graphic designer throughout the launch window.

Phase 7

Handoff & Documentation

Delivered full project documentation: what was migrated, how the content system works, automation logic, and team workflows. Left the client with a running operation rather than a dependency on me to keep things moving.

The Approach
Most projects fail in the handoff. This one was designed for it.

What I do differently when managing a complex, multi-track engagement.

01

Audit before you build

Before any migration or build work started, I documented everything: the full platform stack, all email tags and segments, every existing offer and funnel URL. Nothing moves until it's mapped.

02

Run parallel tracks without dropping balls

Migration, content ops, and launch prep were all running simultaneously. This required clear ownership, sequenced milestones, and a project tracker the entire team could work from — not just me.

03

Build for the team, not just the delivery

Every system I built was documented and explained. The content calendar, approval workflows, and asset folders were designed for a non-technical client to manage independently after the engagement ended.
