02 — The Challenge

A business running on six platforms simultaneously

By the time this engagement started, the client had built their business across a fragmented stack that had grown organically over years. Every tool solved one problem but created new ones.

MemberVault

Course delivery and digital product hosting

Keap (Infusionsoft)

CRM, contact records, automations, and all email

Leadpages

Opt-in pages, landing pages, and sales pages

Squarespace

Separate membership site and content delivery area

WordPress (multiple)

Podcast site, program websites, and Thrive Quiz weighted assessment

Multiple Payment Processors

Stripe direct + Samcart — no unified checkout

The result: high monthly software costs, siloed customer data, inconsistent user experience, and limited automation and reporting capability. The goal was to consolidate everything into GoHighLevel — without disrupting a single live customer.

MemberVault + Keap + Leadpages + WordPress + Squarespace + Stripe → GoHighLevel
03 — Migration Process

A structured approach, before a single file moves

Every migration I run follows the same six-phase process. Most migrations fail not during execution — but because of what wasn't planned before it started.

01

System Inventory

Full audit of every existing asset: all contacts and tags, automations and email sequences, funnels and pages, courses and products, payment integrations, and domain configurations. Nothing gets migrated until it is documented and mapped.

02

Migration Plan & Sequencing

A phased migration sequence designed to protect live customers throughout. High-risk items — active automations, enrolled students, payment flows — are migrated last, after everything downstream has been rebuilt and tested.

03

Build & Rebuild Inside GHL

Each component is rebuilt using GHL's native logic — not just copied over. Where original workflows were messy or brittle, they were simplified and improved before migration. A direct copy carries all the original problems with it.

04

QA & End-to-End Testing

Every funnel, automation, and access flow tested before cutover: test purchases, enrollment confirmations, automation trigger checks, and deliverability verification. Each component signed off before moving to the next phase.

05

Cutover & Stabilization

Domain transfers, DNS updates, and final contact imports staged to minimize disruption. Available post-launch to resolve edge cases quickly — the period immediately after cutover is when issues surface.

06

Documentation Handover

Full documentation delivered to the client: what was migrated, how the system is structured, what each automation does, and how to manage it going forward. The work should be maintainable after I leave.

The Approach
Most migrations just move the mess to a new address.

Here's what I do differently — and why it matters for the business after I'm gone.

01

Rebuild with the destination platform in mind

Forcing Keap's patterns into GHL creates brittle automations. Every workflow was rebuilt using GHL's native logic — cleaner, faster, and maintainable by the client's own team.

02

Simplify before you migrate

If a workflow had logic conflicts in the old system, those problems get fixed before migration — not carried over and left for later.

03

Document what you built

Every migration ends with full system documentation: what was migrated, how it's structured, what each automation does. The client shouldn't need to call me to understand their own system.
