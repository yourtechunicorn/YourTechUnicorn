02 — The Challenge

No system — just a stack of disconnected pieces

When this engagement started, the client had a functioning coaching practice with no automated client journey to support it. Leads came in manually. Discovery calls were tracked informally. Enrollment required back-and-forth emails. Program delivery had no structured onboarding. The business was growing faster than the infrastructure holding it together.

The goal was to build a complete end-to-end system inside GoHighLevel that would take a prospect from their first interaction all the way through enrollment, program completion, and alumni follow-up — without the business owner managing every step manually.

Lead Capture

No unified entry point — no lead magnet, no automated follow-up, no CRM tracking

Discovery Calls

Calendar booking existed but had no reminders, no pre-qualification, no post-call automation

Enrollment

Contracts sent manually — no payment link automation, no login delivery sequence

Program Delivery

Course content existed but had no structured onboarding or automated intake process

Post-Program

No alumni sequence, no testimonial collection, no automated newsletter opt-in

"The business needed infrastructure that could scale without creating a second full-time job for the owner."

03 — Build Process

Architecture first, then execution

Building a client journey system from scratch requires mapping every possible path before writing a single workflow. Where I start every engagement like this.

01

Discovery & Requirements Mapping

Full audit of the business model, client stages, and every touchpoint in the existing (informal) process. Every workflow starts as a flowchart before it becomes an automation — manual steps are mapped first so nothing gets missed in the build.

02

Journey Architecture & Sequencing

Mapped the full client lifecycle: lead entry points, discovery call paths, four post-call outcomes, enrollment chain, and post-program flow. Built the logic structure before a single workflow was created — this is what prevents gaps and double-triggers later.

03

Phased Build Inside GHL

Built in stages, starting with lead capture and working downstream. Each stage was tested before moving to the next. The enrollment chain — payment to contract to login — was built last and only after everything upstream was confirmed working.

04

End-to-End QA & Beta Testing

Every workflow, form, and email sequence tested with real contacts before go-live. Edge cases — multiple form submissions, duplicate email triggers, re-entry scenarios, coupon behavior across payment plans — identified and resolved before handoff.

05

Iteration & Refinement

Beta feedback incorporated in real time. Scoring errors corrected, workflow re-entry logic adjusted, email sequences refined based on what the client observed in testing. The system shipped cleaner because of it.

06

Client Training & Documentation Handoff

12 short tutorial videos recorded covering every routine task the client would need to manage independently. Full system documentation delivered alongside. The goal: the client should never need to call me to run their own system.

04 — Scope of Build

Six systems. One connected journey.

Every component was designed to hand off cleanly to the next. A lead enters, gets nurtured, books a call, and — depending on the outcome — is routed into the right path automatically.

Area 01

Lead Capture & Entry Points

  • Public contact form (website embed) with re-entry protection
  • Admin form for manually referred or internally added leads
  • Lead magnet quiz as a third entry path for cold traffic
  • All three entry points feed into the same CRM pipeline and trigger the same nurture start
Area 02

Scored Lead Magnet Quiz

  • Multi-question assessment with multi-select checkbox scoring
  • Three score tiers — each delivering a personalized result email
  • Post-quiz 3-email nurture sequence to warm leads toward booking
  • Conditional workflow logic routes leads based on calculated score
Area 03

Discovery Call System

  • GHL calendar with pre-qualification form shown inline after booking
  • Automated confirmation email sent immediately on booking
  • Three-step reminder sequence: 24 hours, 1 hour, and 5 minutes before the call
  • Separate automated responses for cancelled calls and no-shows
Area 04

4-Path Post-Call Routing

Business owner moves the contact to a CRM pipeline stage after each call. Each stage triggers a distinct automated path.

YES
  • Payment link sent
  • Contract triggered on payment
  • Login delivered on signature
MAYBE
  • 2-email nurture
  • Discount code included
  • Re-invite to book
NOT YET
  • 2-email nurture
  • Discount code included
  • Future program opt-in
NOT A FIT
  • 2-email sequence
  • Wellness resources
  • Newsletter opt-in
Area 05

Enrollment & Onboarding Chain

  • Payment link delivery triggers contract via GHL Proposals
  • Digital signature confirmed before login is released — no manual checks
  • Membership account created and login credentials delivered automatically
  • Onboarding email sequence starts 1 minute after login delivery
  • Clinic intake form sent and followed up automatically when contact is tagged
  • Discount code logic tested for both one-time and 3-pay plan scenarios
Area 06

Program Delivery & Alumni

  • Course structure built in GHL Memberships with thumbnails and categories
  • Oils onboarding bonus section added for clients with the starter bundle
  • Alumni tag auto-subscribes client to newsletter and future program list
  • Alumni email sequence with testimonial collection and follow-up reminder
  • Testimonial follow-up sent conditionally — only if form not yet submitted

In addition to the six core systems, the engagement included building a newsletter template, rebranding 10+ protocol guides and educational content assets in Canva, and setting up email deliverability infrastructure (SPF, DKIM, DMARC) for the sending domain.

04b — Spotlight: The Enrollment Chain

From "yes" to login — fully automated

The enrollment sequence is where most coaching businesses still rely on manual back-and-forth. The goal here was zero manual touchpoints between a client saying yes and receiving their first email inside the program.

Trigger

Business owner marks contact as YES in the CRM Opportunities pipeline

Step 1

Automated email sent to client with payment link and plan options (one-time or 3-pay)

Step 2

Payment confirmed — coaching agreement (proposal) sent via GHL for digital signature

Step 3

Signature confirmed — GHL membership account created and login credentials generated

Step 4

Login details email sent with portal link and access instructions

Step 5

Onboarding info email sent 1 minute after login delivery

Step 6

Client enters onboarding sequence — weekly check-ins, intake form, and program orientation

Re-entry prevention was applied at each stage of the enrollment chain to prevent clients from receiving duplicate contracts, login emails, or onboarding sequences if they re-submitted a form or triggered a workflow more than once — a common issue in GHL builds that goes unaddressed until something breaks.

The Approach
A system that works the same way whether you're watching it or not.

Here's what I do differently — and why it matters long after the build is done.

01

Map the full journey before building anything

Most automation problems are actually architecture problems. Building before mapping creates gaps, double-triggers, and edge cases that only surface under live traffic. Every engagement starts with a flowchart, not a workflow.

02

Build for edge cases, not just the happy path

Re-entry prevention, duplicate email handling, conditional testimonial follow-ups, coupon logic across different payment plans — the things that matter most are the ones most builds ignore until something breaks with a real client.

03

Hand over a system the client can actually run

Documentation and training aren't an afterthought. Every engagement ends with the client able to manage their own system. If they need to call me to send a newsletter or add someone to a course, the handoff failed.
